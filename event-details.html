Todo está dicho

By Andreas Gursky

06 March - 10 March 2025

Venue: Madrid

Andreas Gursky is a renowned German photographer celebrated for his large-scale, high-resolution images that capture global landscapes, industry, and commerce. His work presents a hyperrealistic view of modern society, blending photography and digital manipulation to emphasize patterns, order, and chaos in contemporary life.

Manolo Valdés

By Ed Ruscha

15 March - 19 March 2025

Venue: Madrid

Ed Ruscha is a leading figure in American Pop Art, known for his striking word paintings and unique interpretations of urban culture. His work often integrates elements of advertising and mass media, challenging perceptions of language, symbols, and the modern American experience.

No puedes evitarlo

By Banksy

22 March - 26 March 2025

Venue: Madrid

Banksy is an anonymous street artist whose politically charged graffiti and murals critique consumerism, war, and social injustice. His work, often infused with irony and dark humor, has gained global recognition, challenging authority and sparking debates about art’s role in activism.

By Ai Weiwei

30 March - 03 April 2025

Venue: Madrid

Ai Weiwei is a Chinese artist and activist whose work confronts issues of censorship, human rights, and cultural identity. His powerful installations, sculptures, and multimedia works use art as a form of protest, challenging political oppression and advocating for social change.
