Todo está dicho

By Andreas Gursky

06 March - 10 March 2025

Venue: Madrid

Andreas Gursky is a renowned German artist celebrated for his large-scale, high-resolution and detailed art work that capture global landscapes, industry, and commerce. His work presents a hyperrealistic view of modern society, blending art and digital manipulation to emphasize patterns, order, and chaos in contemporary life. Gursky’s works, such as 99 Cent and Rhein II, are some of the most expensive art pieces ever sold, cementing his influence in modern days.

Manolo Valdés

By Ed Ruscha

15 March - 19 March 2025

Venue: Madrid

Ed Ruscha is an American artist associated with the Pop Art movement, known for his paintings, photography, and graphic design. Born in 1937, he gained recognition for his text-based artworks, which incorporate words and phrases into bold, minimalist compositions. His works, such as Standard Station and Hollywood, capture the essence of American culture, particularly the landscapes and signage of Los Angeles. Ruscha’s art reflects a blend of humor, nostalgia, and critique, making him a defining figure in postwar American art.

No puedes evitarlo

By Banksy

22 March - 26 March 2025

Venue: Madrid

Banksy is a mysterious and anonymous street artist, political activist, and filmmaker known for his thought-provoking and often satirical graffiti. Emerging in the 1990s, his stencil-based artworks tackle themes of war, consumerism, and social justice, often appearing unexpectedly on public walls across the world. His works, such as Girl with a Balloon and Love is in the Bin, have gained international fame, blurring the lines between street art and high art. Despite his anonymity, Banksy remains one of the most influential and controversial figures in contemporary art.

浮気

By Yayoi Kusama

30 March - 03 April 2025

Venue: Madrid

Yayoi Kusama is a renowned Japanese contemporary artist known for her avant-garde, immersive installations and signature polka dot motifs. Born in 1929, she has worked across various mediums, including painting, sculpture, performance art, and fashion. Her work often explores themes of infinity, self-obliteration, and mental illness, influenced by her personal experiences with hallucinations. Kusama gained global recognition for her "Infinity Mirror Rooms". As one of the most influential artists of the 20th and 21st centuries, her bold and visionary art continues to inspire and challenge perceptions of space and identity.
